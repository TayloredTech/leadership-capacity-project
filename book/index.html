The idea, in one breath

Capacity is not something you either have or do not have. It is a wheel. Four points: your biology, your leadership, your environment, your behaviour. Turned one way, it builds you. Turned the other, it empties you, faster the harder you push.

I spent a year sitting with senior leaders trying to understand why the best ones burn out first. The answer was never willpower. It was this wheel, turning the wrong way, using their own drive against them.

This is not a productivity book. It is a book about the four things that actually decide whether you last.

The mission

My mission is to bring bio literacy to leadership. It is about showing what it actually takes to build a hyper performing team led by someone who can sustain their own momentum, without sacrificing the relationships that matter most: the ones they come home to at night.

Why I am writing this

I have spent my career being hired to bring rigour to founder-led businesses, and I have watched the same pattern more times than I can count. The most capable people in the room are the ones who get spent first. Not because they are weak. Because they care the most.

A while ago I started asking senior leaders a simple question: what does capacity actually mean to you. Fifty conversations later, nobody agreed on the word. But everyone described the same collapse. The same three o'clock, nothing left feeling.

That gap, between the word and the feeling, is where this book started. I am writing it the way I would want to read it. Plain, honest, and grounded in real conversations, not theory.

What the book covers

  • Why your best people burn out first, and why it is not a willpower problem
  • The four point wheel behind capacity: biology, leadership, environment, behaviour
  • Why the wheel you turn does not stop with you. It is the air your team breathes
  • What the leaders who last actually do differently
  • Why the old grind no longer buys anyone anything
